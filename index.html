Climate Change

Global warming

What is global warming?

The continual rise in the average global temperature and its consequences for the climate system on Earth are known as global warming. The rate of increase in the average world temperature is currently faster than in the past.

What is causing global warming?

Human activity is the main cause of global warming. Carbon dioxide and methane are two of the main greenhouse gases that are increased by the use of fossil fuels, deforestation, and some industrial and agricultural practises.

Why do greenhouse gases increase global warming?

Some of the heat that the Earth radiates after being warmed by sunlight is absorbed by greenhouse gases. More of these gases trap more heat in the lower atmosphere of the Earth, contributing to global warming.When greenhouse gases are released into the atmosphere, they act as insulation, keeping the heat from the sun from going back out into space. This means that there is more heat in the lower atmosphere of Earth, contributing to climate change. This means that the more CO2, CH4 and N2O we release, the warmer and warmer the Earth will become.

Impacts

In addition to heat waves and wildfires being more frequent, deserts are growing larger. Permafrost melting, glacier retreat, and a reduction in sea ice have all been impacted by increased heat in the Arctic. Storms, droughts, and other weather extremes are becoming more intense as a result of rising temperatures. Numerous species are being forced to move or go extinct due to the rapid environmental change occurring in the Arctic, coral reefs, and mountains.

The most affected areas are those near deserts, and on coastlines that will be flooded if we don't do anything about it.

How can global warming be managed

  • Keep fossil fuels in the ground.
    • We need to stop using fossil fuels—not only are they limited, they are also very damaging for the environment.
  • Invest in renewables.
    • We can't stop using fossil fuels if we have nothing to replace it with, we need to invest in fossil fuels now!
  • Switch to sustainable transport.
    • Cars are bad for the environment. We should all switch (if we can) to using a bus/a bike/our feet to get around.
  • Insulate homes.
    • Heating costs are much higher than they have to be. If we insulate more homes, we can save energy and the environment.
  • Replant forests.
    • Trees are one of the main carbon sinks. They take in CO 2 and release O 2. If we can replant the forests we lost due to deforestation, we can reduce the amount of CO 2 in the atmosphere.
  • Protect the oceans.
    • The ocean absorbs lots of CO 2, it's important to protect it from over-fishing and drilling.
  • Reduce consumption.
    • Reducing consumption of products in wealthy countries can help put less strain on the planet.
  • Reduce plastic.
    • Plastic production is a carbon-intensive process that contributes to emissions and is expected to account for 17% of the global carbon budget by 2050.

Climate Change Denial

One other opinion held by some is climate change denial. Climate change is a refutation of research about climate change, its effects on nature, and humans. This can also happen when people accept the science but fail to understand it. These beliefs are very harmful considering how urgent and important action on climate change is. Climate change denial is often the product of oil & gas companies creating fake organisations and studies in order to muddy the waters of scientific consensus on climate change.

Economy

A lot of investment will be needed to solve the climate crisis, but this doesn't mean that going green has to ruin the economy.

Shifting to a green economy could yield a direct economic gain of $26 trillion through 2030 compared with business-as-usual. This could produce over 65 million new low-carbon jobs. -UN

By the year 2030, we need to invest ~$90tn on infrastructure. This is a lot, but, for every $1 we invest in good infrastructure, we can get $4 back. Also, we can save up to $17tn if we build our cities in a smart and efficient way.
